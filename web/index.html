Tutorial: plate with a hole

Walk through a complete analysis end to end — from CAD geometry to a result you can check against textbook theory. The classic stress-concentration problem takes about a minute in the browser.

Import the geometry

Launch the solver and drag in a STEP file of a plate with a central hole. KoFEM tessellates the CAD surface with OpenCASCADE so you see the real shape in the viewport.

Constrain one edge

In Constraints, pick the left face and fix it. You can hold all three directions, or just one — a single-DOF roller for a symmetry plane is fully supported.

Apply the tension load

Select the opposite face and add a load pulling the plate in tension. Enter the total force; KoFEM distributes it across the face nodes.

Mesh the volume

Click Mesh to fill the solid with tetrahedra using Netgen's OpenCASCADE integration, refining automatically around the hole where the stress gradient is steep.

Solve

Run the MFEM linear-elastic solve. Displacements and von Mises stress come back in seconds and colour the model — the peak ring sits right at the edge of the hole.

Check against theory

Read the peak stress and divide by the applied tension. For a wide plate the analytical concentration factor is Kt = 3 (Kirsch) — KoFEM lands within ~10% on a coarse mesh, and closer as you refine.
